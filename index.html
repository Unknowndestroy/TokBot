Transparency

What We Collect

  • User IDs and Discord Usernames to maintain the blacklist system.
  • When a ban occurs, the bot saves the Server ID where the event triggered to help staff investigate raids and take a deeper dive into specific scams.
  • We store specific message content or Message ID that triggered a detection (e.g. a scam link).
  • We store image hashes of scam images. We do not store the original images directly, only their mathematical hashes for future detection.
  • For users flagged as "Hacked", we temporarily store a list of your roles. This allows us to restore your permissions once your account is secured and the appeal is granted.
  • Any notes added by staff members during a ticket or appeal are stored to ensure consistent moderation.

How We Use Your Data

  • Data is shared across the servers to block known bot accounts and scammers instantly.
  • We track detection counts (e.g. how many scams were blocked today) to monitor performance. These stats do NOT identify individual clean users.
  • Your Ban ID v2 XXXX-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX is used to link your appeal ticket to your specific case in the database.
  • Ticket conversations are archived in our database for quality assurance.
  • Our database is private and accessible only by authorized Bot Developers and high-level Administrators.
  • Users who have never triggered a security flag do not have personal data stored in our permanent ban databases.
